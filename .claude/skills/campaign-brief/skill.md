# Campaign Brief

Generate a structured campaign brief to align all stakeholders.

## Usage

```
/campaign-brief
```

## Process

1. **Gather Context**
   - What are we promoting? (product, feature, event, brand)
   - Who is the target audience?
   - What is the primary goal? (awareness, leads, sales, retention)
   - What is the budget?
   - What is the timeline?
   - Any constraints? (brand, legal, technical)

2. **Define Strategy**
   - **Objective:** Primary goal with measurable success criteria
   - **Audience:** Demographics, psychographics, pain points
   - **Positioning:** Key message and differentiation
   - **Channels:** Where will this run? (paid search, social, display, email)
   - **Creative Approach:** Tone, format, hero visual direction
   - **KPIs:** 3-5 measurable metrics

3. **Draft Brief**
   Present a structured brief with:
   - Campaign name
   - Objective and KPIs
   - Target audience (persona)
   - Key message and supporting points
   - Channel strategy and budget allocation
   - Creative requirements
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Success metrics

4. **Get Approval**
   Wait for user sign-off before delegating execution.

## Output Template

```markdown
# Campaign Brief: [Campaign Name]

## Objective
[Primary goal in one sentence]

**Success Criteria:**
- KPI 1: [metric] reaches [target] by [date]
- KPI 2: [metric] reaches [target] by [date]
- KPI 3: [metric] reaches [target] by [date]

## Target Audience
**Primary Persona:** [Name/Type]
- Demographics: [age, location, role, etc.]
- Psychographics: [motivations, pain points, goals]
- Current behavior: [how they currently solve this problem]

## Positioning
**Key Message:** [One sentence value prop]

**Supporting Points:**
1. [Reason to believe #1]
2. [Reason to believe #2]
3. [Reason to believe #3]

**Differentiation:** [What makes this unique vs. competitors]

## Channel Strategy
| Channel | Budget | Goal | Format |
|---------|--------|------|--------|
| Paid Search | $X | [goal] | [format] |
| Social Ads | $X | [goal] | [format] |
| Display | $X | [goal] | [format] |

**Total Budget:** $X

## Creative Requirements
- **Tone:** [formal, casual, playful, urgent, etc.]
- **Format:** [video, static, carousel, stories, etc.]
- **Hero Visual:** [concept description]
- **CTA:** [primary call-to-action]

## Timeline
- **Brief Approval:** [date]
- **Creative Review:** [date]
- **Campaign Setup:** [date]
- **Launch:** [date]
- **Optimization Reviews:** [weekly/biweekly]
- **Campaign End:** [date]

## Risks & Constraints
- [Risk or constraint #1]
- [Risk or constraint #2]

## Next Steps
1. [Creative Lead] Design ad concepts
2. [Media Lead] Set up campaigns
3. [Analytics Lead] Configure tracking
```

## Follow-Up Actions

After brief approval:
- Share with Creative Lead for concept development
- Share with Media Lead for channel setup
- Share with Analytics Lead for tracking configuration
- Schedule kick-off meeting

## Common Pitfalls

- **Too many KPIs** — Pick 3-5 max, aligned to single primary goal
- **Vague audience** — "Everyone" is no one. Be specific.
- **No differentiation** — If positioning sounds like competitors, refine it
- **Unrealistic timeline** — Account for review cycles (strategy, creative, legal)
- **Budget mismatch** — If budget is too low for stated goals, flag it immediately

## Remember

A great brief makes everything else easier. Invest time here to save time later.
