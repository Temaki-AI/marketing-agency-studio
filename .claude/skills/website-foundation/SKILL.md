# Website Foundation

Build your core website pages using brand positioning and StoryBrand framework.

**This is Level 2.** You need a website before you can publish content or run campaigns.

---

## Core Pages

We'll build:
1. **Homepage** - Your front door (most important)
2. **Product/Service Page** - What you offer
3. **About Page** - Who you are (and why it matters)
4. **Contact Page** - How to reach you

Optional (but recommended):
5. **Pricing Page** - Transparent pricing builds trust
6. **Resources/Blog Hub** - Where content lives

---

## Page 1: Homepage

**Structure (based on StoryBrand):**

### Section 1: Hero (Above the Fold)
**Headline** - What success do they get?
- Not: "We're the leading provider of..."
- Yes: "Launch campaigns without missing deadlines"

**Subhead** - Clarify or add detail
- 1 sentence, expand on headline
- "Marketing managers at B2B companies stay organized with [Product]"

**CTA** - Direct call to action
- Primary: "Start free trial"
- Secondary: "Watch demo"

**Hero Image/Video**
- Show product in use (not abstract)
- Happy customer succeeding (not stock photo)

Ask the user:
- What success does your customer get? (1 sentence)
- What clarifying detail matters? (1 sentence)
- What's your primary CTA?

---

### Section 2: Stakes (The Problem)
**Show them you understand their struggle.**

Load from `brandscript.md`:
- External problem
- Internal problem (emotion)

**Structure:**
"Without [solution], you're stuck [describing their current pain]."

3 pain points (keep brief):
- ❌ [Pain 1]
- ❌ [Pain 2]
- ❌ [Pain 3]

**Emotion lives here.** Make them feel understood.

---

### Section 3: Value Proposition (The Solution)
**How you help (not features, benefits).**

Load from `brandscript.md`:
- Your role as guide (empathy + authority)

**Structure:**
"[Product] helps [customer] [achieve goal]."

3 key benefits (not features):
- ✅ [Benefit 1]
- ✅ [Benefit 2]
- ✅ [Benefit 3]

**Social proof:** Logo bar or testimonial

---

### Section 4: The Plan (How It Works)
**Make it simple. 3 steps.**

Load from `brandscript.md`: Process plan

**Structure:**
"Here's how it works:"

1. **[Step 1 title]** - [1 sentence description]
2. **[Step 2 title]** - [1 sentence description]
3. **[Step 3 title]** - [1 sentence description]

**Confusion kills sales.** Make this brain-dead simple.

---

### Section 5: Explanatory (More Details)
**For people who need more info.**

Choose 3-4 of:
- **Feature spotlight** - Key product capabilities
- **Use cases** - "Great for [scenario]"
- **Integrations** - What you connect with
- **Customer stories** - 2-3 quick wins
- **FAQ** - Address top objections

Don't overwhelm. Be selective.

---

### Section 6: Success Vision
**Paint the picture of their life after.**

Load from `brandscript.md`: Success (status, completeness, external)

**Structure:**
"Imagine: [aspirational but believable future state]"

"With [Product], you'll:"
- [Success outcome 1]
- [Success outcome 2]
- [Success outcome 3]

**Make it emotional.** This is the dream.

---

### Section 7: Final CTA
**Repeat the call to action.**

Same as hero section:
- Primary CTA (direct)
- Secondary CTA (transitional)

**Assume they scrolled the whole page and forgot what you sell.** Remind them.

---

## Page 2: Product/Service Page

**If you have multiple offerings, create one page per product.**

### Structure:

**Hero Section**
- Headline: [Product] helps [customer] [achieve goal]
- Subhead: [Clarify what it does]
- CTA

**Problem → Solution**
- What problem does THIS product solve?
- How does it solve it? (high level)

**Features → Benefits**
- Not: "Has advanced analytics"
- Yes: "Know exactly which campaigns are working"

List 4-6 key features with benefit-driven copy:
- **[Feature name]** - [What it lets them do]

**Use Cases**
- "Great for [scenario 1]"
- "Perfect when [scenario 2]"

**Proof**
- Customer testimonial specific to this product
- Stats/results

**CTA**
- "Try [Product] free"
- "Schedule demo"

---

## Page 3: About Page

**Nobody cares about your origin story. They care about how you help them.**

### Structure:

**Hero**
- Headline: "We're on a mission to [help customer achieve goal]"
- Not: "Founded in 2015, we are..."

**Empathy (Guide)**
- Show you understand their struggle
- "We've seen too many [customers] struggle with [problem]"
- Personal story (brief) that connects you to them

**Authority (Proof)**
- Stats: customers served, years in business, results delivered
- Team: who we are (if it builds trust)
- Values: what we believe (if it differentiates)

**Transition to Product**
- "That's why we built [Product]"
- Link to product page or demo

**CTA**
- "See how we can help"
- "Schedule a call"

**Key insight:** About page is still about THEM, not you.

---

## Page 4: Contact Page

**Make it easy. Remove friction.**

### Structure:

**Headline**
- "Let's talk" or "Get in touch"

**Contact Form**
- Name (required)
- Email (required)
- Company (optional but useful for B2B)
- Message (required)
- Submit button: "[Action] →" (e.g., "Send message →")

**Alternative Contact Methods**
- Email: [address]
- Phone: [number] (if you want calls)
- Address: [if physical location matters]
- Hours: [response time expectations]

**Social Links** (if relevant)
- LinkedIn (B2B)
- Twitter/X
- Not Facebook unless consumer brand

**Reassurance**
- "We typically respond within 24 hours"
- "Your info is never shared"

---

## Optional: Pricing Page

**Transparency builds trust.** If you can show pricing, do it.

### Structure:

**Headline**
- "Simple, transparent pricing"

**Pricing Tiers** (if applicable)
- Starter, Professional, Enterprise
- Show monthly/annual toggle
- Highlight most popular

**Each Tier:**
- Price (obvious, large)
- Target customer ("Great for [persona]")
- Key features (4-6 bullets)
- CTA per tier

**FAQ Below Plans**
- "Can I change plans?"
- "What happens if I cancel?"
- "Do you offer discounts?"

**Comparison Table** (if complex)
- Feature-by-feature comparison

**If Complex Pricing:**
- "Starts at [base price]"
- "Custom quote" CTA
- Transparency > confusion

---

## Optional: Resources/Blog Hub

**If you'll publish content, give it a home.**

### Structure:

**Header**
- "Resources" or "Learn" or "Blog"
- Subhead: What they'll find here

**Featured Post**
- Latest or most important article
- Large image, headline, excerpt
- "Read more →"

**Category Filters**
- Guides
- Case Studies
- Industry News
- How-Tos

**Post Grid**
- 6-9 recent posts
- Image, headline, excerpt, "Read →"
- Pagination or "Load more"

**Newsletter Signup**
- "Get weekly marketing insights"
- Email field + Subscribe button

## Notes

- Homepage is MOST important - spend time here
- Use BrandScript as source of truth for messaging
- Keep copy concise (people skim)
- Every page needs a CTA
- Mobile-first (most traffic is mobile)
- Page speed matters (compress images, minimize bloat)
- Accessibility matters (contrast ratios, alt text, semantic HTML)
- This is COPY blueprint, not design mockups (but design notes included)
- Budget 90-120 minutes for full website blueprint
